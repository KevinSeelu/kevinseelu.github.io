About

An engineer who likes solving problems at the hardware level — where signals are real and the stakes are physical.

I'm a B.Tech student in Applied Electronics & Instrumentation Engineering at Rajagiri School of Engineering and Technology, Kochi. My work lives at the intersection of embedded systems, hardware design, and real-world instrumentation.

I've had the opportunity to work at International Aerospace Manufacturing Private Limited — a HAL and Rolls Royce joint venture — gaining hands-on exposure to precision aerospace component manufacturing for Rolls Royce engines. I've also interned at Unique World Robotics, programming Dobot Magician robotic arms and developing embedded firmware.

On the project side, I've built a fully automated Water Quality Index sensing system, a WiFi-based intrusion detection system, and an analog RF signal detector — all hardware, all real. I'm also actively working on VLSI projects in Verilog and grinding LeetCode daily.

WiFi Room Intrusion Detection System

IoT-based intrusion detection using ESP8266 NodeMCU and HC-SR04 ultrasonic sensor, achieving ±0.3cm detection accuracy within a 343cm range.
